Introduction

Prions diseases are incurable and stem from the abnormal folding of the PrPC protein, which results in the creation of prion. The PrPC protein is a signal glycoprotein that is located on the outside of neuron cells. This protein, when created and folded normally, aids with certain cell functions and prevents cell death from external factors. Prions are infectious in the absence of nucleic acids and can lead to cell death, causing a neurodegenerative disease. Approximately one in one million people will contract this disease every year - 7,000 people in the world every year. The fatality rate is 100%.
